Custom-Size Air Filter Subscriptions — The Complete Guide

Approximately 15–20% of American homes use HVAC filter sizes that are not available at local hardware stores. The causes range from custom ductwork built by non-standard contractors to older home retrofits to imported HVAC systems. Whatever the reason, non-standard filter sizes create a consistent supply challenge: either drive to a specialty HVAC supply house, order online and wait, or — worst — use the closest available standard size and accept the air bypass gaps it creates.

Custom-size filter subscription services solve this problem permanently. Set up once, specify your exact dimensions, choose your MERV rating, and receive correctly-sized filters on your preferred schedule. The premium over standard sizes is typically modest, and the convenience of never searching for your filter again is significant.

How to Measure Your Custom Filter Correctly

Getting the measurement right is critical — a filter that is even 0.5 inches too large or too small may not fit properly or may have air bypass gaps. Here is the exact measurement process used by HVAC professionals:

  • Step 1: Remove the existing filter from your system. If it has any size information on the frame, photograph it before disposal.
  • Step 2: Measure the existing filter's actual dimensions with a tape measure: length × width × depth. Record these numbers.
  • Step 3: This is your 'actual size.' The nominal size you order is typically the actual size rounded up to the nearest 0.5 or 1 inch.
  • Step 4: If you have no existing filter, measure the filter slot opening directly: the clear opening width, height, and depth.
  • Step 5: Verify by ordering one filter before subscribing. Confirm the fit is correct before setting up automatic delivery.
  • Step 6: Once confirmed, set your subscription schedule based on your home's particle load and MERV rating.

Pricing Guide — What Custom Filters Actually Cost

Custom-size filter pricing follows a straightforward logic: manufacturing cost plus a custom-cut premium. For sizes that are close to standard (e.g., 17x25 vs 16x25), the premium is small because media can be cut from a standard sheet. For truly unusual sizes that require special cutting, the premium is larger.

Managing Multiple Custom Sizes in One Home

Homes with multiple custom sizes are more common than you might expect: different size returns in different areas, a combination of main system and mini-split filters, or a home addition with different duct sizing. Managing multiple custom sizes under one subscription account:

  • Register each size separately in your subscription account. Most services allow unlimited size-schedule combinations.
  • Set different delivery frequencies for different locations — high-traffic areas may need monthly; low-traffic zones every 90 days.
  • Label each filter location on a diagram (or in your phone notes) to prevent mix-ups during installation.
  • Consider ordering a small buffer stock — if delivery is delayed, you want a spare for each size.
  • Review all sizes annually — ductwork modifications, new HVAC units, or moved equipment may change what you need.

The Easiest Way to Set Up a Custom Filter Subscription

The three-step process to correctly set up a custom filter subscription: (1) Measure every filter location in your home, photograph existing filters if present. (2) Order one filter in each size to confirm fit before subscribing. (3) Once confirmed, subscribe at your target interval. Most services allow you to modify sizes within your account at any time, so if measurements change, the subscription can be updated without starting over.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where can I get a subscription for a custom-size air filter?

Second Nature, FilterBuy, and Nordic Pure all offer custom-size filter subscriptions. FilterBuy has the widest selection at 600+ sizes. Custom filters typically cost 20–40% more than standard sizes.

What counts as a non-standard HVAC filter size?

Any filter size not in the top 20 most common residential sizes. Examples: 17x22x1, 19x21x1, 15x20x1, 18x24x1. If your local hardware store does not stock it, it is a non-standard size.

How do I measure for a custom filter if the existing filter has no label?

Measure the filter slot opening: length x width x depth. Round each measurement UP to the nearest 0.25 inch. This is your actual size. For the nominal size you order, round up again to the nearest 1 inch.

How long does a custom-size filter subscription take to deliver?

Standard sizes in an unusual dimension typically ship within 1–3 business days. Truly custom cuts (non-standard sizes with custom MERV media) may take 3–7 business days.

Are custom-size filters the same quality as standard sizes?

Yes — custom-size filters use the same media and construction as standard sizes, just cut to your specified dimensions. MERV rating and filter life are equivalent to the same media in a standard size.

Can I get MERV 13 in a custom size?

Yes — FilterBuy and Nordic Pure both offer MERV 13 in custom sizes. Second Nature offers MERV 11 custom. Expect 10–20% premium over their standard MERV 13 pricing.

What if I need multiple different custom sizes for my home?

Most subscription services allow multiple sizes under one subscription. Add each size you need, set individual delivery schedules for each, and pay per-filter. Consolidating to one service simplifies the process.

Is it worth ordering custom filters vs finding the closest standard size?

Always order the correct custom size. Using a close-but-not-exact standard size leaves gaps that bypass filtration. A correctly-sized custom filter at a 25% premium is far better than a slightly-wrong standard size.
